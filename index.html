Types of Hong Kong Company Structures

Selecting the right legal structure is a strategic decision. It directly impacts your tax position, liability exposure, operational flexibility, and ability to scale.

The most widely used structure for Hong Kong company formation. It offers limited liability, full foreign ownership, and strong global credibility, making it the preferred choice for international businesses.

Key Requirements

  • Minimum 1 shareholder (individual or corporate)
  • Minimum 1 director (any nationality)
  • Mandatory local company secretary
  • Registered office address in Hong Kong
  • Minimum share capital starting from HKD 1
  • Maximum 50 shareholders

Key Advantages

  • Limited liability protection for shareholders
  • Separate legal entity from its owners
  • 100% foreign ownership permitted
  • Eligible for offshore profits tax exemption
  • Easy transfer and issuance of shares
  • Strong banking acceptance and global credibility

A branch office allows an overseas company to establish a presence in Hong Kong without creating a separate legal entity. It operates as an extension of the parent company.

A representative office is used for market research and business development. It cannot generate revenue or enter into commercial contracts.

Documents Required for Hong Kong Company Formation

A well-prepared documentation process is essential for smooth incorporation and banking approval. Requirements vary depending on whether shareholders are individuals or corporate entities.

Our team assists with all documentation requirements

For Individual Shareholders and Directors

  • Certified copy of passport (all relevant pages)
  • Proof of residential address such as a utility bill or bank statement issued within the last 3 months
  • Professional profile such as CV, resume, or LinkedIn

For Corporate Shareholders

  • Certificate of Incorporation of the parent company
  • Memorandum and Articles of Association
  • Register of directors and shareholders
  • Board resolution approving Hong Kong incorporation
  • Declaration of beneficial ownership and UBO structure
  • Latest audited financial statements

Company Formation Documents

  • Completed incorporation form (NNC1)
  • Articles of Association
  • Proposed company name (English and/or Chinese)
  • Registered office address in Hong Kong
  • Appointment and consent of company secretary

Post-Incorporation – Bank Account Requirements

  • Certificate of Incorporation
  • Business Registration Certificate
  • Board resolution for account opening
  • Business plan and description of activities
  • Expected transaction flow and counterparties
  • KYC documents for all directors, shareholders, and signatories
